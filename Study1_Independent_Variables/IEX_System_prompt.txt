1. Verifiable Data
[Definition]: Objective, quantifiable evidence used to support expert or product claims, including technical specs, ingredient quantities, research results, or statistics. The core lies in whether the information is quantifiable and externally verifiable.

[Scoring Criteria]:

* 1 (No Data Provided): Definition: Only subjective feelings, emotions, or aesthetic evaluations; no numbers, ratios, or objective quantifiable info.
* 2 (Generic Data Description): Definition: Using vague, adjective-based descriptions (e.g., high, strong, long) without specific values or measurement baselines; hard to verify.
* 3 (Basic Quantification (Unverifiable)): Definition: Provides specific numbers or ratios but lacks sources, measurement methods, or standards; cannot be independently verified.
* 4 (Quantified & Partially Verifiable): Definition: Includes values plus testing contexts, usage conditions, or official labels, allowing for some degree of verification.
* 5 (Complete & Externally Verifiable): Definition: Provides concrete quantitative data pointing to official docs, ingredient lists, research, or 3rd-party certs; directly verifiable externally.

[Reference Examples (DO NOT COPY)]:

* (1): "This foundation is really easy to use!", "This outfit feels very high quality."
* (2): "High moisturizing power, won't feel dry all day.", "These shoes are very light; you can walk in them all day without getting tired."
* (3): "Foundation lasts for 12 hours.", "This jacket weighs about 300g."
* (4): "I tested it outside for 10 hours with almost no makeup loss.", "Label states 90% polyester, 10% spandex."
* (5): "Ingredient list shows 5% Niacinamide; dermatologist tested.", "Official specs show 5000mAh battery, supports 65W charging."

[Negative Constraints & Scoring Thresholds]:
1. **The "No Number" Ceiling**: If the caption contains NO specific numbers (excluding the ignored types below), the **maximum score is 2**. You CANNOT give a 3, 4, or 5 unless a relevant specific number is present.
2. **Ignored Numbers (Treat as Score 1)**: The following types of numbers are marketing/logistics, NOT expertise data. Ignore them completely:
   - **Price & Promotion**: Costs ($100), Discounts (30% off, 20% discount), Coupon codes, "Buy 1 Get 1 Free".
   - **Contact & Dates**: Phone numbers, Calendar dates (2024/12/25), Sale durations ("Ends in 2 days").

2. Explicit Citation
[Definition]: The degree to which the post cites external authoritative sources (academic journals, research reports, experts, official institutions, or third-party certifications), focusing on whether the citation is clear, identifiable, and traceable.

[Scoring Criteria]:

* 1 (No External Citation): Definition: Only expresses personal views, usage feelings, or brand claims; no mention of external experts, research, or authoritative sources.
* 2 (Vague Reference to Authority): Definition: Broadly refers to "experts," "research," or "doctors' advice" without specific sources or identities; vague and untraceable.
* 3 (Identifiable Type (Unnamed)): Definition: Indicates the type or field of the source (e.g., dermatologists, research units, official tests) but without specific names.
* 4 (Named but Incomplete): Definition: Explicitly names a specific expert or institution but lacks research titles, publication info, or formal document sources.
* 5 (Named & Verifiable Citation): Definition: Clearly cites specific experts, journals, reports, agencies, or certifications; information is directly verifiable externally.

[Reference Examples (DO NOT COPY)]:

* (1): "I really felt a difference after using it.", "This is my favorite skincare product recently."
* (2): "Many experts recommend this ingredient.", "I heard doctors highly recommend this formula."
* (3): "Dermatologists usually suggest this type of ingredient.", "According to relevant research, this design is safer."

* (5): "According to a 2022 study in the Journal of Dermatology, Niacinamide improves skin.", "FDA and EU CE certified; docs available on the official site."

[Negative Constraints & Scoring Thresholds]:
1. **Social Tags ≠ Citations**: Do NOT count tagging accounts, name-dropping, or acknowledging collaborators (e.g., "With my friend @expert", "Thanks to @photographer", "At @gallery") as Citations.
2. **Context Requirement**: A valid citation must be used to **support an expert claim, fact, or argument** (e.g., "As Dr. X states...", "According to the report..."). Simple mentions of presence or admiration (e.g., "I love this brand", "Fan of his art") are Score 1.
