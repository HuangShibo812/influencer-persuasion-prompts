────────────────────────────────────────
【SECTION 1: CONCEPTUAL BOUNDARY — WHAT IS IMAGE VALENCE (NOT QUALITY)】
────────────────────────────────────────

**Image Emotional Valence (IMV)** is defined as a viewer's overall emotional pleasantness response to an image — the degree to which the visual stimuli evoke a positive (pleasant, warm, joyful) vs. negative (unpleasant, gloomy, uncomfortable) affective state.

This construct follows the human annotation protocol established by Yu et al. (2026, MIS Quarterly), in which trained raters evaluated each image holistically by asking:
→ "How positive or negative does this image make you feel?"
Raters were instructed to assess the OVERALL felt emotion evoked by the image as a whole — not to decompose it into sub-features.

**This is NOT visual quality (VPQ).** Do NOT confuse:
* Visual Quality = How technically and aesthetically well-crafted the image is (sharpness, focus, composition skill, post-processing)
* Emotional Valence = What overall FEELING the image triggers in the viewer

A technically poor photo of a person laughing genuinely in warm sunlight = HIGH valence.
A technically flawless product photo on a sterile white background = NEUTRAL valence (Score 3).

**This is NOT arousal.** Do NOT confuse:
* Arousal = How activating or exciting the image is (energetic vs. calm)
* Valence = Whether the overall feeling is pleasant or unpleasant

A calm, serene beach at sunset = HIGH valence (Score 4), LOW arousal.
Score valence ONLY. Ignore activation level entirely.

────────────────────────────────────────
【SECTION 2: THE GOLDEN RULES】
────────────────────────────────────────

1. **SCOPE CONSTRAINT — IMAGE ONLY**:
   * Evaluate ONLY what is visually present in the image.
   * Do NOT read any caption, text overlay, hashtag, or brand name in the image to infer sentiment.

2. **HOLISTIC IMPRESSION RULE (CRITICAL)**:
   * This is a SINGLE holistic score — not a checklist of visual features.
   * Simulate the felt response of an average viewer encountering this image for the first time.
   * Ask yourself: "As I look at this image, what is my immediate overall emotional reaction — does it feel pleasant or unpleasant, and to what degree?"
   * Do NOT score individual visual elements separately and average them. Judge the gestalt impression.

3. **VIEWER-CENTERED JUDGMENT**:
   * Judge from the perspective of an average viewer — not an art critic or technical photographer.
   * Focus on the spontaneous emotional impression, not deliberate analysis of technique or composition.

4. **ANTI-HALLUCINATION RULE**:
   * Do NOT assume valence from brand identity, product category, or cultural associations.
   * Do NOT assume a luxury or beauty brand = positive valence.
   * Score only what the image EXPLICITLY and VISUALLY communicates.
   * If no emotional cues are present → Score MUST be 3.

────────────────────────────────────────
【SECTION 3: SCORING STANDARDS】
────────────────────────────────────────

**IMV: Image Emotional Valence**
[Definition]:
Rate the overall degree of pleasantness or unpleasantness that the image evokes in an average viewer upon first sight. This is a felt, holistic response — not a technical assessment.

[Core Scoring Question per Level]:

* **1 (Strongly Negative / Unpleasant)**:
"This image makes me feel clear discomfort or want to look away."
The image evokes an unmistakable sense of unease, distress, or negative affect. The dominant visual experience is actively unpleasant — not merely cold or uninviting, but genuinely uncomfortable.
  * Example signals (non-exhaustive): Oppressive darkness with no visual relief; expressions of pain, distress, or anger dominating the frame; chaotic, threatening, or visually disturbing scenes; environments that feel unsafe, dirty, or deeply unwelcoming.

* **2 (Mildly Negative / Cold and Uninviting)**:
"This image makes me feel cold, detached, or slightly unappealing."
The image evokes mild discomfort or emotional coldness. The overall impression leans unpleasant or noticeably flat and uninviting, though not actively distressing.
  * Example signals (non-exhaustive): Predominantly cold, desaturated, or harsh tones with no counterbalancing warmth; blank or tense expressions paired with an unwelcoming setting; sparse, sterile, or isolating scenes that feel emotionally distant.
  * **Critical boundary with Score 3**: A technically poor product shot that simply lacks quality does NOT automatically become Score 2. Score 2 requires the image to actively evoke a feeling of coldness, distance, or mild unease — not merely the absence of positivity.

* **3 (Neutral / No Emotional Response)**:
"This image makes me feel nothing in particular — neither comfortable nor uncomfortable."
The image evokes no clear positive or negative emotion. The viewer feels neither pleased nor displeased — a sense of complete indifference, as if looking at a product specification sheet.
  * Typical pattern: Clean white-background product shots with no lifestyle context; flat-lay arrangements without affective cues; visually functional images that communicate information but evoke no feeling.

* **4 (Mildly Positive / Warm and Pleasant)**:
"This image makes me feel slightly comfortable, warm, or visually at ease."
The image evokes a gentle sense of warmth, comfort, or aesthetic pleasure. The viewer has a natural, mild positive reaction — the feeling of "this looks nice" or "this feels good" without strong excitement.
  * Example signals (non-exhaustive): Warm or soft tones that feel inviting; relaxed, natural expressions or a gentle smile; pleasant lifestyle scenes such as cozy interiors, soft daylight, or natural settings; a general sense of visual comfort and gentleness.
  * **Critical boundary with Score 5**: Score 4 is "naturally comfortable." Score 5 is "noticeably joyful." If the positive feeling requires no deliberate analysis but is not strongly joyful → Score 4.

* **5 (Strongly Positive / Joyful and Uplifting)**:
"This image makes me feel noticeably happy, joyful, or emotionally uplifted."
The image evokes a strong, unmistakable positive emotional response. The viewer feels clearly uplifted upon first sight — the emotional warmth or joy is immediate and requires no deliberate analysis.
  * Example signals (non-exhaustive): Genuine, authentic smiling or laughing expressions; vibrant, harmonious colors that feel celebratory; visually rich scenes communicating happiness, energy, or celebration; an overall emotional "brightness" the viewer cannot ignore.

────────────────────────────────────────
【SECTION 4: NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS】
────────────────────────────────────────

1. **The "Pure Product Shot" Rule**:
   * If the image contains ONLY a product on a neutral or white background, with no lifestyle context, no human presence, and no affective color work → Score MUST be 3.
   * A technically poor product shot that simply lacks quality also scores 3 — NOT 2. Score 2 requires active emotional coldness, not merely the absence of quality.

2. **The "No Assumption" Rule**:
   * NEVER assign Score 4 or 5 based solely on inferred product category or brand prestige.
   * NEVER assign Score 1 or 2 based solely on a dark artistic aesthetic if the overall impression is not genuinely unpleasant or cold.
   * A moody, dark-toned fashion editorial that feels "cool and intentional" but not uncomfortable → Score 3, NOT Score 1 or 2.
   * Score 5 requires a clearly visible, authentic, full smile (teeth showing or cheeks visibly raised) OR a scene with multiple simultaneous strong positive signals (vibrant saturated colors + joyful expressions + celebratory context). A gentle or reserved smile with positive context = Score 4. A broad, open, unambiguous smile = Score 5.

3. **The "Carousel Grid" Rule**:
   * If the image is a carousel grid of multiple sub-images, assess the unified overall emotional impression across all sub-images.
   * Do not let one outlier sub-image dominate the final score. Weight toward the majority impression.

4. **The "Score Inflation" Rule**:
   * Given that this dataset consists of commercial endorsement posts, do NOT default to Score 4 simply because the image is a brand post.
   * If the image is technically adequate but emotionally flat (e.g., a product held up against a plain wall with a neutral expression) → Score 3, NOT Score 4.

5. **The "Cold Isolation" Rule**:
   * A vast empty background, a product or subject appearing isolated or diminished in a cold, minimal setting, or a direct unsmiling gaze against a stark white or grey background → these actively evoke emotional distance. Score MUST be 2, NOT 3. Score 3 is reserved for scenes that feel genuinely indifferent — not scenes that actively feel cold or isolating.
6. **The "Semantic Override" Rule**:
   * Do NOT let conceptual associations (e.g., 'parent + child = heartwarming', 'CGI character = artificial') override what the image visually communicates. Judge only the visual impression. If a parent-child scene is shot from far away with a grey sky and cold winter tones, the visual impression may be 4 or lower — not 5.
